Mashed Potatoes

Mashed potatoes, simple, fluffy, and so delicious.

This recipe will take you through the steps to make the best, most basic, but well-made mashed potatoes. You'll want to bookmark this recipe and save for later. Of course, with all good recipes, it can always be taken with a grain of salt and prepared the way you like it.

This is more of a guideline than it is a recipe, please feel free to adjust based on your preferences and tastes. Season and flavor to your liking. In this recipe, I will also give reasonings for adjustments in the recipe.

Ingredients

  • 4 lbs russet or yellow potatoes, peeled, whole

  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted

  • 2 cups heavy cream, light cream, or whole milk

  • 1 1/2 cups parmesan cheese, grated

  • 1/8-1/4 cup of garlic powder

  • Optional: 1 cup chives, freshly chopped

  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions

  1. Bring a pot of water to boil. Add potatoes until tender but firm. This will take about 15 minutes. Drain the water.

  2. While the potatoes are cooking, in a small saucepan and heat the butter and milk until milk has taken on a pale yellow color.

  3. In a seperate bowl, mash potatoes until there are no more large chunks of potato left. Do not over-mash the potatoes or the potatoes will become gluey.

  4. Slowly add the milk and butter mixture into potatoes and fold to desired fluffiness.

  5. Add the parmesan cheese and garlic powder(optional) to the potatoes.

  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  7. Sprinkle some chopped chives on top to add some color contrast, if you'd like!

Stuffing

Stuffing, another popular Thanksgiving staple that will add a fragrant aroma to any plate.

The only recipe you'll ever need for as long as you're cooking for Thanksgiving. Golden and crunchy outside, soft and savory inside, this recipe is wildly flavorful and buttery. This recipe brings a wonderful, fragrant aroma and neutral flavor that will go with anything you can find on the table.

Disclaimer: this recipe will contain butter, and lots of it. But what's a holiday feast without gaining a few pounds, right?

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 loaves bread, cubed, preferably toasted or stale

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted

  • 3 cups sweet or yellow onion, diced. about 2 large onions

  • 2 cups celery, diced

  • 6 cloves garlic, minced

  • 3 tbsp fresh sage, chopped

  • 3 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped

  • 3 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped

  • 2 1/2 cups chicken or veggie stock

  • 2 eggs, large

  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350F. Brush a 9x13 baking dish with melted butter, olive oil, or spray with non-stick spray. Place the bread in a large mixing bowl and set aside.

  2. Heat some butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Once melted, stir in onion, celery, and garlic with a big pinch of salt and peper - at least 3/4 teaspoons of each. Cook until onions and celery have become soft and translucent.

  3. Then, stir in the chopped sage, parsley, and rosemary. Set some aside for later to sprinkle. Cook this until you can really smell the herbs. Next, stir in a cup of stock.

  4. Pour the onion and celery mix into the bowl of bread cubes and toss to coat the bread crumbs.

  5. In a small bowl, whisk the remaining 1 1/2 cups of stock and the 2 eggs.

  6. Pour that mixture into the bread cubes and stir and fold until thoroughly combined.

  7. Bake the stuffing for about 45-50 minutes, until the internal temperature reads 160F. If it starts getting too brown, you can lay some foil over it.

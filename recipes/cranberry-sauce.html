Cranberry Sauce

Cranberry sauce, sweet, tangy, a strong contrast to any Thanksgiving Staple

This recipe will take you through the steps to make the best cranberry sauce you'll ever have.

This is more of a guideline than it is a recipe, please feel free to adjust based on your preferences and tastes. Season and flavor to your liking. In this recipe, I will also give reasonings for adjustments in the recipe.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs russet or yellow potatoes

    • The standard for preparing mashed potatoes, these are the best for mashing. But don't mash too much or you'll end up with glue!

  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter

    • Depending on how buttery you want your potatoes, this can be adjusted to your liking.

  • 1 cup Heavy cream, light cream, or whole milk

    • Whichever dairy product you use, adjust the recipe to the desired consistency. You may wish to use more if you feel like the recipe doesn't include enough.

  • About 1/2 to 1/3 cup grated parmesan cheese

    • This will increase the saltiness of the mashed potatoes so taste the mashed potatoes at this point and season accordingly.

  • 1-2 tbsp of garlic powder

    • Adds a bit of depth to the mashed potatoes, you don't have to use it but I like it. Adjust to taste if used.

  • Salt and pepper, to taste

    • Self-explanatory. Always season your food!

Directions

  1. Bring a pot of water to boil. Add potatoes until tender but firm. This will take about 15 minutes. Drain the water.
  2. While the potatoes are cooking, in a small saucepan and heat the butter and milk until the butter is fully melted.
  3. In a seperate bowl, mash potatoes until there are no more large chunks of potato left. Do not over-mash the potatoes or the potatoes will become gluey.
  4. Slowly add the milk and butter mixture into potatoes and fold to desired fluffiness.
  5. Add the parmesan cheese and garlic powder(optional) to the potatoes.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
